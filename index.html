The Challenge

The program had previously attempted to build a systems integration laboratory twice and failed both times. I joined in October 2023 with a mandate to succeed where others had failed. The challenge was to create a production-ready real-time analytics platform supporting multi-modal data streams (physiological, eye-tracking, simulation, survey data) with millisecond-level latency requirements for live performance monitoring.

Additionally, three separate organizations (DCS, Army Research Laboratories, and government partners) had conflicting approaches to data architecture. Without alignment, the platform couldn't deploy. The laboratory needed to open in February 2024—just 4 months away.

My Role & Approach

I served as single-threaded owner with end-to-end accountability for scope, execution, and outcomes. I defined success criteria, performance targets, and evaluation requirements, translating high-level objectives into a structured execution roadmap.

Key responsibilities included:

  • Defining platform performance thresholds: millisecond-level latency for physiological data, strict cross-pipeline synchronization, quantified data-loss tolerances
  • Coordinating 6 engineering teams across internal organizations with independent roadmaps (simulation, orchestration, data engineering, analytics, research)
  • Resolving critical 3-org architectural conflict blocking deployment through direct negotiation and technical alignment
  • Making final go/no-go decision for Data Validation Pilot and approving all datasets as analysis-ready

Technical Solution

Kafka Architecture

Designed and implemented Apache Kafka-based data infrastructure with 7 concurrent pipelines processing ~40 topics, supporting real-time streaming analytics and synchronized multimodal data.

Performance Gates

Established explicit thresholds: millisecond latency for physiological data, cross-pipeline synchronization accuracy, acceptable data-loss rates (<30%). All metrics documented and enforced.

Common Data Model

Negotiated and delivered unified data architecture across DCS, ARL, and government partners. Now mandated as reusable standard for all future programs.

Results & Impact

  • Successfully launched laboratory in February 2024 after two prior failed attempts, establishing it as the foundation for all evaluation programs
  • Validated platform under operational conditions with 20 participants during week-long Data Validation Pilot (July 2024)
  • Demonstrated real-time analytics and visualization capabilities enabling live performance monitoring (previously impossible)
  • Platform now supports 4 teams executing all evaluation programs, enabling 4 major evaluation events since deployment
  • Data architecture mandated as reusable standard across 3 organizations (DCS, ARL, GVSC), with all platform components remaining in active use
  • Enabled first ML-assisted performance benchmarking, establishing foundation for advanced analytics capabilities
  • Recognized for delivery impact through promotion from Systems Engineer to Technical Program Manager (Feb 2024) and equity incentive following primary technical authorship of advanced analytics components for winning $70.7M Army simulation contract (GVSL)
